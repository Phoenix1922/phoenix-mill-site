Our Story

        

History of Phoenix Mill

        

          From Ford “Village Industry” plant to a restored historic event venue in the heart of Hines Park.         

      
    
    
      
        

From Factory to Event Venue

        

          Phoenix Mill was originally built for Ford Motor Company as part of its innovative “Village Industries”           program. The plant produced parts for early Ford vehicles and later supported the World War II effort.         

        

          After decades of industrial use, the building eventually sat vacant until it was carefully restored and           reimagined as Phoenix Mill Events. Much of the original architecture, character, and history remain visible           throughout the space.         

        

The Ford “Village Industries” Concept

        

          Rather than building every factory in Detroit, Henry Ford located small industrial plants along rivers and in           rural communities. These “Village Industries” used local water power and provided jobs close to home for           residents who otherwise might have needed to move to the city.         

        

Women in the Workforce & The Arsenal of Democracy

        

          During World War II, Phoenix Mill and similar factories became part of the “Arsenal of Democracy,” producing           parts and components that supported the Allied war effort. Many women joined the workforce in these plants,           taking on new roles and helping to power the production boom.         

        

Phoenix Mill Today

        

          Today, Phoenix Mill Events honors that legacy while offering a modern, comfortable space for weddings,           corporate gatherings, social events, and community celebrations. Original industrial details and river views           create a backdrop that is both historic and unforgettable.         

        

          We are proud to preserve the history of Phoenix Mill while giving it new life as a place where guests create           memories of their own.
